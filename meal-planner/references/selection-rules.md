# Weekly Selection Rules

## Objective

Generate a week that is:
- practical for one-person home cooking
- balanced across proteins and vegetables
- stable enough to persist for the week
- varied across weeks instead of repeating the same template

## Daily structure

Each day contains:
- one breakfast template
- one lunch main dish
- one side dish or soup
- one dinner reuse note

Default interpretation:
- lunch is the main cooked meal
- dinner reuses lunch
- breakfast stays simple
- daily recommendation should be rendered from saved weekly state, not freshly re-planned every day

## Main-dish rule

Main dishes should be real lunch anchors.

Prefer:
- chicken, beef, pork, fish, shrimp mains
- dishes that reheat reasonably well
- dishes that fit weekday cooking time limits

Avoid as default mains:
- sauces or bases
- egg-only dishes
- obviously thin vegetable dishes
- obvious noodle/rice-bowl entries when they weaken the two-dish lunch structure
- feast dishes or awkward shell-heavy weekday dishes

Tofu-led mains are exceptions, not the default.

## Side-dish rule

Side dishes should complement the main and improve meal completeness.

Prefer sides that add:
- leafy vegetables
- cruciferous vegetables
- fungi
- fresh contrast after heavy mains
- soup support when the main is dry or spicy

## Weekly balance

Protein rotation target:
- chicken about 2 days
- fish/shrimp about 2 days
- beef about 1-2 days
- pork about 1 day

Vegetable diversity target:
- leafy vegetables multiple times
- fungi at least once
- colorful vegetables multiple times
- fresh/light sides after heavy or spicy mains

## Cross-week variety

The planner should:
- penalize recently used mains
- penalize recently used sides
- avoid repeated exact dishes in the same week
- avoid adjacent-day same-protein repetition when possible

## Shopping output

Weekly shopping should summarize:
- proteins
- fresh vegetables
- durable vegetables
- breakfast staples
- fruit / small extras

It should not behave like detailed pantry inventory tracking.
